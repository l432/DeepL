Dear Oleg,


Thank you very much for your message and for your interest in contributing to the SMART project.


STAB VIDA’s Doctor Vida Education device is already at the prototype stage. It has been designed to perform absorbance, fluorescence, and chemiluminescence measurements in an affordable and accessible way, using basic optics and sensors. Within this project, our goal is to bring it closer to the market and broaden its educational applications.


Given your group’s expertise in physics and photonics, I believe you could make a valuable contribution by helping us validate and further improve the device’s optical and sensing performance - both at the hardware level and through software and algorithmic approaches. In particular, this could include applying physics formulas or models to enhance signal quality, reduce noise, correct for background or drift, and improve quantitative accuracy of the measurements, and this could then even be explained to students.


Additionally, you may also contribute to the design of a simple quality control and calibration kit or protocol, suitable both for manufacturing use and for students, to ensure reliable spectroscopy measurements. Such kits, possibly based on off-the-shelf standard materials, could be very useful for physics and instrumentation students, just as biology or biochemistry students would use the device for molecular biology or biochemical applications.


Finally, given your experience in characterizing semiconductors, another potential educational application could be to use our device to actually characterize LEDs and other semiconductor components (if this makes sense to you). I believe the “chemiluminescence” mode would be particularly suitable for this, since in that mode only the device’s spectral sensor is active and can measure any incoming light within the sensor spectrum range. This approach could even be extended to IR LEDs, as our sensor is also capable of detecting IR radiation.


I hope these ideas are useful. I do see strong potential for your contribution along these lines, though please do confirm this with Prof. Capelo, as he is the current proposal coordinator.

Best regards,

Gonçalo
